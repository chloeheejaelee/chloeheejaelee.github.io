What is Android?

  • Android is a touchscreen mobile operating system based on a modified version of the Linux kernel and other open source software, built primarily for smartphones and tablets. Google commercially sponsors Android, which is created by a group of developers known as the Open Handset Alliance. The majority of Android versions are proprietary. The Android Open Source Project (AOSP), which is free and open-source software (FOSS) largely distributed under the Apache License, provided the essential components. When Android is installed on a device, the ability to modify the normally FOSS software is frequently limited, either by not giving the source code or by blocking reinstallation through technical means, thereby making the installed version proprietary. Most Android devices come pre-installed with additional proprietary software, the most notable of which is Google Mobile Services, which comprises important programs like Google Chrome, the digital distribution platform Google Play, and the Google Play Services development platform. The source code has been used to create Android variations for a variety of other devices, including gaming consoles, digital cameras, portable media players, and personal computers, each with its own user interface. Android TV for televisions and Wear OS for wearables, both created by Google, are two well-known versions. On Android, software packages in the APK format are typically distributed through proprietary app stores such as Google Play Store, Amazon Appstore, Samsung Galaxy Store, Huawei AppGallery, Cafe Bazaar, and GetJar, as well as open source platforms such as Aptoide or F-Droid. Since 2011, Android has been the most popular smartphone and tablet operating system in the world. It has over three billion monthly active users as of May 2021, the largest installed base of any operating system, and the Google Play Store has over three million apps as of January 2021. The most recent version, Android 12, was launched on October 4, 2021.

Advantages

  • Android is an open-source platform allowing UI customization: Android is an open source operating system that developers can change to build customized User Interface. App developers are able to build application for this plateform and change them to customized forms. iOS does not allow this and is strict on how their core code is presented.

  • Supports cloud storage enabling sync of devices with G-account: Because Google created Android, users are able to connect their Gmail account to have access to a larger cloud storage.

  • Continual improvement & removal of old features: Since Google Android has a huge community of developers and users, it helps create a large continous check on the codes and features. This helps bring in better modifiations and alterations to bring better upgrades all the time. It is more faster to keep user favorite features and remove the older unwanted ones.

  • Supports 3rd party widget & information display on screen: Android gives users freedom to download 3rd party widgets and display their content on the home screen.

  • Supports running multiple apps simultaneously: Android allows multiple apps to run simultaneously compared to iOS.

  • Expandable memory & runs on affordable large devices: One of the biggest advantages of using dvices running on Android plateform is that it supports expandable memory expansion by adding memory card to the phone. Users of this plateform enjoy the privilege of storing e-books, music, videos and games on their devices.

  • Wide range of devices to choose from: Android consumers have a wide range of smartphone devices to choose from at various price points. When it comes to Android smartphones, there is something for everyone, regardless of their budget. Almost every company creates devices that run on the Android platform, providing customers a variety of choices when it comes to purchasing. This is extremely restricting for Apple users, who are forced to utilize just the company's pricey equipment!

Not only does it run slow because of low hardware but it also comes iwth several default apps that further slow down the smartphone operation.
